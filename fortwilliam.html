Ben Nevis: August 1 2020

At last we made it, we were always going to reach the top this time after I backed out at the half way Lochan back in 2018. That had been an abysmally dreich day and I really wanted a good view and scenery for Alex's first time up Ben Nevis. Alas it wasn't to be and I guess it just shows that you have to take what you get and make the most it, the little river crossing made up for it a bit but I was disappointed at not making it. This time though no such problem, picked Alex up early in Dundee then up the A9 and over to Torlundy to park in the North Face car park and off up the path towards the Sinclair Memorial Hut and the Alt a Mhuilinn burn tumbling down out of the Corrie Leis. We quickly moved south-west and end up following quad bike tracks up to the Lochan where we joined the tourist path. Setting a ferocious pace, I paced myself in the wake of the natural athlete that is Alex, as we moved into the clouds and up the Z bends. Good steady walking saw us on the summit, without a view, but a tremendous variety of people. One party in hoodies and leisure pants we found celebrating with cans of tennents. Easy walk back to the car park and then an amazing fish and chips in Dunkeld before back to Edinburgh for the night.

Ben Nevis with John and Isla June 30 2015

This turned into one of these lovely chilled weekends, we must have driven up in the morning and then simply worked our way up the tourist path. Simple is not really the wors though and it amazes me just how many people manage to get themselves up to the top every day, every week and every month. It is a constant stream of people marching up and down, even at night you can see the head torches bobbing about. We made good, warm, time up to the lochan, 3.5 hrs, and a bite to eat but it is steep and really relentless. We kept a steady pace, still too slow for the gazelle that is John who bounded up effortlessly, up the Z bends and then it kind of caught up with us and it was a bit of a slow trudge over the rocky summit for the perfect photo opportunity at the top. It was great to be up here with John and Isla, sharing one of my greatest love of the outdoors. It was a relentless trek down mixed in with one or two moments of "hanger", but we made it, sneeked Lui into the Youth Hostel and scoffed some Fish n Chips.

CMD Arrette and Ben Nevis September 2009:
Storer Route 24

Wow, one of those days when it all just comes together in a perfect storm of sunshine, timing and scenery. An absolutely stunning day out. After climbing the tourist path we followed the path round to the halfway lochan then traversed round to North side and climbed very steep slopes to Carn Mor Dearg, which gave us perfect views of Ben Nevis and the CMD Arrette.

Ring of Steall August 2nd 2011

Visits to Fort William are a rare treat for me, living as I do on the East Coast. Most trips have involved mountain bikes and weekends away to events such as 10 Under the Ben, Relentless 24 or Funduro style cycling. The Witch's Trails are well pisted trail centre routes with lots of whoops and fun. Oviously the hillwalking is also fantastic.
