Ben Chonzie August 28 2020

I'm sure this hill has a claim to fame but i'm not sure what it is. I think it is the lowest Munro ? and, unsurprisingly, it does not figure in Ralph Storer's book. Just took the chance to go and as ever with the hills it turned out really well. It's a good 2.5 hour drive over to the start but I set off with Lui and Lilly and headed off on dreich morning, up into the mist. Wonderful to see a red squirrel at the bird table in the cottage garden and really had a wonderfully relaxed stroll along the river before following the track uphill onto the hill. Very steady and pleasant tramping about a further 2 miles saw me on the summit, where the mist cleared slightly for some lovely views over the moor to the dam at Loch Turrett. Very easy walking saw me retracing my steps and a chat with a chap who had heard the call of Plover? I thought it was a Dotteral's call :-) and quite a lengthy chat with the Gamey who told me all about the short eared owls and Kites nesting that year. Lovely day out and the Heather was gorgeous and vivid bright purple. I'm sitting typing this listening to Gypsy Manouche, and drinking a Black Stump Red so I think this is a very interesting read!!!!

Over Loch Lomand to Arrochar and Crianlarich

Ben Lomand August 14 2020. Storer Route 2

What an absolute Scorcher of a day, once we got up above the clouds at ca. 2000m. Bit of a brutal climb to be honest, straight up, straight down a real leg basher. After squeezing the car into a tight spot next to Loch Lomand we cracked on up the steep sides, up through the cloud to reach the less severe slopes and bright sunshine. It was very pleasant round the top over grassy slopes with the cloud sitting in all the corries and a lovely sense of being up there away from the hustle and bustle. Very busy on top and lovely to so sit and have lunch with fantastic views west to The Cobbler and north to Crianlarich. Stunning. Quick blister fix before steep, rocky steps down and round to Ptarmigan Hill and steady track back to the Lochside. Time for a spot of dinner at Rowardennan before collecting Ruth at Glasgow airport and back to Dundee.

Hope we get above this!

Autumn's Glory

Ben Cruchan September 26 2020: Storer Route 13

This was a long overdue walk, route 13 in Storer has been marked for at least thirty years, since I went walking with Jim Macdonald in the Golden “PhD” Years of the late eighty’s early ninety’s. A good forecast and a very early start got me the 3 hours over to the Cruachan layby by 6.45am with blue skies and a fresh wind. My friend Sarah G. had just recently died of a brain tumour, unbelievable really and obviously quite a shock, but such a reminder to just get outdoors and do the things you enjoy while you can. Brutal climb straight from the car, up steeply wooded slopes before opening up onto the access road after a good 45 min of sweating and scrambling. Once it opened up though it was clear views and walking round the east side of the reservoir gave access to a steady climb up to the Lairig Torran. Excellent views and a grand place to stop for a “dump with a view” and a spot of breakfast. Let me cool down a bit before reaching the windy ridge and comfortable walking up gentle slopes over the top of Stob Garbh and onto the summit of Stob Diamh at 3274ft. This was a great photo opportunity and most of the shots of the ridge and Cruachan were taken from here. From then on it really was a pleasure to be out in a high and rugged place with just steady walking along a windy ridge over to Cruachan and its neighbours. Quite busy actually and the constant calling of the shepherd / gamekeeper seemed to keep everyone entertained with his wild shouts and cursing echoing around the corrie. I declined the opportunity of Drochaid Ghlas, where I met a chap who had driven 5 hours to come walking today, impressive but reminds me to JFDI!! Skirting around some steep, rocky aprons I reached the summit of Ben Cruachan by about 11.00. Magnificent, clear air, spectacular views just soak it up!! Took the opportunity of continuing round to the sister summit Stob Dearg and keeping up on the high ground. Lovely conditions for a day out, knee jarring descent however down to the side of the Loch and then again down through the trees back to the car. A very satisfying day out.
